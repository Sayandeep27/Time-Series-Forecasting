## 1️⃣ What is Stationarity?

**Stationarity** means that the statistical properties of a time series remain **constant over time**.

In simple words:

> A time series is stationary if it behaves in a stable and consistent way over time.

---

## 2️⃣ Why is Stationarity Important?

Most time series models such as **ARIMA** assume that the data is stationary.

If data is not stationary:

* Patterns change over time
* Model cannot learn effectively
* Predictions become unreliable

---

## 3️⃣ Key Properties of Stationary Time Series

A time series is stationary if the following conditions hold:

### 🔹 Constant Mean

* The average value does not change over time
* No upward or downward trend

### 🔹 Constant Variance

* The spread or variability remains constant

### 🔹 Constant Autocovariance

* Relationship between values depends only on lag, not time

---

## 4️⃣ Stationary vs Non-Stationary

| Feature        | Stationary | Non-Stationary    |
| -------------- | ---------- | ----------------- |
| Mean           | Constant   | Changes over time |
| Variance       | Constant   | Changes over time |
| Trend          | Absent     | Present           |
| Seasonality    | Absent     | Present           |
| Predictability | High       | Low               |

---

## 5️⃣ Types of Stationarity

### 🔹 Strict Stationarity

* Entire distribution remains unchanged over time
* Rare in real-world data

### 🔹 Weak Stationarity (Most Important)

* Mean is constant
* Variance is constant
* Autocovariance depends only on lag

---

## 6️⃣ Why Trend Breaks Stationarity

Consider a time series with a positive trend:

```
10, 20, 30, 40, 50
```

* Early values are small
* Later values are large

👉 Mean is increasing over time

So:

```
Mean (first half) ≠ Mean (second half)
```

Hence, the series is **non-stationary**.

---

## 7️⃣ Variance and Autocorrelation Discussion

### 🔹 Variance

Even if spread looks constant:

* It is measured around a changing mean
* Therefore, not truly constant

### 🔹 Autocorrelation

In a trending series:

* Early values relate to small values
* Later values relate to large values

👉 Relationship depends on time → not stationary

---

## 8️⃣ Mathematical Intuition

A time series can be represented as:

```
Y_t = Trend_t + Noise_t
```

Where:

* Trend_t → Non-stationary component
* Noise_t → Stationary component

Thus:

👉 Entire series becomes non-stationary due to trend

---

## 9️⃣ How to Make a Time Series Stationary

### 🔹 1. Differencing

```
Y_t' = Y_t - Y_{t-1}
```

Removes trend

---

### 🔹 2. Log Transformation

```
Y_t' = log(Y_t)
```

Stabilizes variance

---

### 🔹 3. Detrending

* Remove trend using regression

---

### 🔹 4. Seasonal Adjustment

* Remove repeating seasonal patterns

---

## 🔟 Summary

* Stationarity = constant statistical properties over time
* Required for most forecasting models
* Key checks:

  * Constant mean
  * Constant variance
  * Constant autocovariance

---

## ✅ Final Key Insight

> A time series must look statistically identical at any point in time to be considered stationary.
