# 📊 Time Series Analysis

Time Series Analysis is a statistical technique used to analyze data points collected or recorded at different points in time. The goal is to understand patterns in the data and often to forecast future values.

---

## 1. What makes it “time series”?

Unlike normal data (like rows in a table), time series data has a **time order**, for example:

* Daily stock prices
* Monthly sales
* Hourly temperature

The order matters because **past values influence future values**.

---

## 2. Key Components of Time Series

A time series is usually made up of these parts:

| Component              | Description                                         | Example                          |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **Trend**              | Long-term direction                                 | Sales increasing every year      |
| **Seasonality**        | Repeating patterns at fixed intervals               | Ice cream sales higher in summer |
| **Cyclic Pattern**     | Long-term fluctuations (not fixed like seasonality) | Economic cycles                  |
| **Noise (Randomness)** | Irregular fluctuations                              | Sudden unexpected spikes/drops   |

---

## 3. Simple Example

Let’s say you track monthly sales of a store:

| Month | Sales |
| ----- | ----- |
| Jan   | 100   |
| Feb   | 120   |
| Mar   | 150   |
| Apr   | 130   |
| May   | 170   |

### What you observe:

* Sales are generally increasing → **Trend**
* If every year December spikes → **Seasonality**

---

## 4. Real-Life Example

Imagine Amazon sales data:

* Higher during Diwali / Christmas → **Seasonality**
* Increasing year by year → **Trend**
* Sudden drop due to market crash → **Noise**

---

## 5. Why Time Series Analysis is Important

It helps in:

### 🔮 Forecasting

* Sales prediction
* Stock price prediction

### 📦 Planning

* Inventory management
* Demand forecasting

### 📈 Understanding Patterns

* Business growth
* Customer behavior

---

## 6. Common Methods Used

Some popular techniques:

* Moving Average
* Exponential Smoothing
* ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average)
* LSTM (Deep Learning)

---

## 7. Very Simple Intuition

Think like this:

> **Time Series Analysis = "Learning from the past to predict the future"**
