# 📊 Stationarity in Time Series

---

## 📌 What is Stationarity?

**Stationarity** means that the statistical properties of a time series do not change over time.

### Simple Explanation

A time series is **stationary** if its behavior is stable and consistent over time.

> **One-Line Intuition:**
> **Stationarity = “The past behaves like the future”**

---

## 🔑 Key Properties of a Stationary Series

A time series is **stationary** if the following conditions are satisfied:

| Property       | Explanation                                                                                  |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Mean**       | The average value remains constant over time                                                 |
| **Variance**   | The spread or fluctuations remain constant                                                   |
| **Covariance** | Relationship between values depends only on lag (distance between observations), not on time |

---

## 👀 Visual Intuition

### ✅ Stationary Data (Stable Behavior)

* Fluctuates around a constant mean
* No trend
* No seasonality
* Consistent variance

### ❌ Non-Stationary Data (Changing Behavior)

* Shows upward or downward trend
* Has seasonality (repeating patterns)
* Variance may increase or decrease over time

---

## 📊 Examples

### ✅ Stationary Examples

* Random noise data
* Daily temperature fluctuations around a constant average (short period)

### ❌ Non-Stationary Examples

* Stock prices (trend over time)
* Sales data with growth trend
* Population growth data

---

## 🚀 Why Stationarity is Important

Most time series models (like **ARIMA**) assume stationarity because:

* It makes data **predictable**
* Patterns become **consistent**
* Model performance improves significantly

---

## 🔧 How to Make Data Stationary

If your data is **non-stationary**, you can transform it using the following techniques:

### 1️⃣ Differencing

Subtract the previous value from the current value:

```math
Y'_t = Y_t - Y_{t-1}
```

---

### 2️⃣ Log Transformation

Used to stabilize variance (especially when variance increases over time):

```math
Y'_t = log(Y_t)
```

---

### 3️⃣ Removing Trend & Seasonality

* **Detrending** → Remove long-term trend
* **Deseasonalizing** → Remove repeating seasonal patterns

---

## 🧠 Quick Summary

| Type               | Description                                  |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Stationary**     | Stable mean, variance, and pattern over time |
| **Non-Stationary** | Changing behavior over time                  |

---

## 📌 Final Intuition

> A time series is stationary when its statistical behavior does not change over time.

---

## 💡 Key Takeaway

* Stationarity is **essential** for time series modeling
* Non-stationary data must be **transformed** before applying models like ARIMA

---

## 🏁 Conclusion

Understanding stationarity is the **foundation of time series analysis**.
Without it, most forecasting models will give unreliable results.
