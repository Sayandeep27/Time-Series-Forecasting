## 1️⃣ Introduction

Time series forecasting predicts future values using past information.

While AR models use **past values**, the **Moving Average (MA) model** uses **past errors (noise/shocks)**.

---

## 2️⃣ What is Moving Average (MA)?

**Moving Average (MA)** means:

> The current value depends on **past forecast errors (random shocks)**.

### 🔹 Simple Idea

* Unexpected shocks from the past affect the present
* The model reacts to **previous prediction errors**

---

## 3️⃣ MA(1) Mathematical Model

The simplest MA model is MA(1):

Yₜ = μ + εₜ + θ εₜ₋₁

### 🔹 Where:

| Symbol | Meaning                            |
| ------ | ---------------------------------- |
| Yₜ     | Current value                      |
| μ      | Mean (constant)                    |
| εₜ     | Current error (white noise)        |
| εₜ₋₁   | Previous error                     |
| θ      | Coefficient (impact of past error) |

---

## 4️⃣ Example Dataset (with Errors)

We’ll use a small time series and assume known errors for illustration:

| Time (t) | Actual Value (Yₜ) | Error (εₜ) |
| -------- | ----------------- | ---------- |
| 1        | 10                | 1          |
| 2        | 12                | -1         |
| 3        | 11                | 2          |
| 4        | 14                | -2         |
| 5        | 13                | 1          |

---

## 5️⃣ Step-by-Step MA Calculation

### 🔹 Assumptions

* μ = 10
* θ = 0.5

### 🔹 Model Used

Yₜ = 10 + εₜ + 0.5εₜ₋₁

---

### 🔹 Calculation Table

| Time (t) | εₜ | εₜ₋₁ | Predicted Yₜ = 10 + εₜ + 0.5εₜ₋₁ |
| -------- | -- | ---- | -------------------------------- |
| 1        | 1  | —    | —                                |
| 2        | -1 | 1    | 10 + (-1) + 0.5(1) = 9.5         |
| 3        | 2  | -1   | 10 + 2 + 0.5(-1) = 11.5          |
| 4        | -2 | 2    | 10 + (-2) + 0.5(2) = 9           |
| 5        | 1  | -2   | 10 + 1 + 0.5(-2) = 10            |

---

### 🔹 Detailed Calculations

* For t = 2 → 10 + (-1) + 0.5 × 1 = 9.5
* For t = 3 → 10 + 2 + 0.5 × (-1) = 11.5
* For t = 4 → 10 + (-2) + 0.5 × 2 = 9
* For t = 5 → 10 + 1 + 0.5 × (-2) = 10

---

## 6️⃣ Interpretation of Results

* The model reacts to **past shocks (errors)**
* A positive past error increases current value
* A negative past error decreases current value

---

## 7️⃣ Key Intuition

👉 If there was an unexpected increase yesterday → today adjusts accordingly

👉 If there was an unexpected drop yesterday → today compensates

### 🔹 Important Insight

* MA models rely on **past errors, not past values**
* Captures **shock or randomness effects**

---

## 8️⃣ Real-World Applications

MA models are used in:

* 📈 Financial market volatility
* 📊 Economic shocks analysis
* 🌦 Weather irregularities
* 🛒 Demand fluctuations

---

## 9️⃣ Summary

| Concept  | Description                               |
| -------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| MA Model | Uses past errors to predict current value |
| MA(1)    | Uses 1 previous error                     |
| Formula  | Yₜ = μ + εₜ + θ εₜ₋₁                      |
| Nature   | Captures randomness/shocks                |

---

## 🚀 Final Takeaway

> **Moving Average models capture the impact of unexpected shocks in time series.**

They complement AR models and are essential for building ARMA/ARIMA models.
